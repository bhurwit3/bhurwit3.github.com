"I am the slow, long-legged Sleepyman and I love you sheep in Persia, California, Argentine, Australia, or Spain - you are the thoughts that help me when I, the Sleepyman, lay my hands on the eyelids of the children of the world at eight o'clock every night - you thousands and thousands of sheep in a procession of dusk making an endless multitudinous drumming on the hills with your hoofs."

-Carl Sandburg

SHEEP - the peaceful, docile, pastoral companion of Christ and the Sandman. Yet Sandburg's Ode omits but one continent where these creatures range. During the early nineteenth century, the introduction of one sheep in particular, the Merino, would have profound and lasting consequences on the colony and the people of South Africa. These ungulates surely made waves, forever changing the landscape, the economy, and the society of South Africa.
