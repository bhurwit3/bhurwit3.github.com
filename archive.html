An Account of Three Journeys from Cape Town into the Southern Parts of Africa

by Francis Masson

Francis Masson was the first plant collector sent abroad by Sir Joseph Banks, the driving force behind Kew Royal Botanical Gardens. In 1772, Masson sailed to South Africa on the HMS Resolution with Captain Cook. Over his three year tour he was able to collect over 500 species of plant.

Warden, Grant Considerations on the State...

Considerations on the State of the Colonial Currency and Foreign Exchanges

by Grant Warden

Grant Warden was an economist who wrote extensively about colonial currency and other financial matters. This work appeared as the Colonial Government was in the process of phasing out the old Dutch Rixdollar. This proved to be a controversial matter, as many Dutch farmers felt they were cheated in the exchange. Unlike many of his contemporaries, Warden was favorable in his depiction of Dutch residents at the Cape.

Kolben, Peter The Present State...

The Present State of the Cape of Good Hope

by Peter Kolben

Peter Kolben was a German traveler who visited the Cape Colony when it was still very sparsely populated and underdeveloped. Kolb is unique in his sympathetic depiction of the native Khoisan people. He was a harsh critic of Dutch slavery, and his account details the torture and other depredations inflicted upon servants and slaves.

Semple, Robert Walks and Sketches...

Walks and Skerches at the Cape of Good Hope

by Robert Semple

Semples account is compiled from journal entries detailing his daily walks, liberally doused with social commentary. Typical of British writers during the first occupation of the Cape Colony, Semple is harshly critical of the Dutch settlers. He mimics common tropes about Dutch materialism, cruelty, and the corrupting influence of Slavery.

Narrative of a Residence in South Africa

by Thomas Pringle

Thomas Pringle was a Scottish immigrant who became the poet laureate of Settlers at the Cape. Pringle was a liberal who campaigned against slavery, and also fought for democratic institutions and freedom of the press. This earned him the ire of Governor Charles Somerset, who was notoriously Autocratic.

 English 1850
Sparrman, Anders A Voyage to the Cape of Good Hope

Anders Sparrman was a Swede and a student of Carl Linnaeus. Sparrman traveled extensively through rural South Africa, addressing both botanical and cultural matters. His account of the Cape was probably read more widely than any other in Europe. Sparrman was critical of the Dutch and their cruel treatment of slaves; many of Sparrman's opinion would be mirrored by later travelers to the Cape.

 Swedish 1789
Lichtenstein, Hinrich Travels in Southern Africa

Unlike most travelers, Lichtenstein was trained as a zoologist, rather than a botanist. During the Batavian Republic, Lichtenstein served as the personal physician to the Cape Governor. Upon his return to Europe, Lichtenstein held positions at the University of Berlin, the Berlin Zoological Museum, and the Royal Swedish Academy of Sciences.
